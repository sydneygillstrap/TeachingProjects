First Grade Adventures!

The Pros & Cons of Interning

The Sunshine & Rainbows: Pros!

Hands-On Experience

You'll gain invaluable, real-world experience in classroom management, lesson planning, and child development. It's learning by doing, not just reading from a book!

Pure Joy & Fun

First graders are enthusiastic, curious, and hilarious. Their energy is contagious, and you'll witness those magical "aha!" moments when a child learns something new.

Build Your Skills

Develop patience, creativity, and communication skills. You'll learn to explain concepts in dozens of different ways until they click for every student.

Professional Growth

You'll build relationships with experienced teachers who can mentor you and write letters of recommendation for your future career.

The Spilled Glitter: Cons

It's Tiring!

Keeping up with a room full of 6 and 7-year-olds is physically and mentally exhausting. There is very little downtime during the school day.

Emotional Rollercoaster

You'll manage everything from scraped knees and hurt feelings to friendship squabbles. It requires a lot of emotional regulation and empathy.

The "Icky" Stuff

From runny noses to bathroom accidents and a constant supply of germs, you'll need a strong stomach and good hand-washing habits!

Limited Authority

As an intern, you're not the lead teacher. You'll have to follow their rules and classroom management style, even if you don't always agree with it.

The Sparkle Summary

  • Is it for you? An internship is the BEST way to decide if teaching young children is your true passion.
  • Resume Booster: It provides powerful, tangible experiences that look amazing on a resume for any career involving education or child care.
  • Expect the Unexpected: Every day is different. Flexibility and a sense of humor are your most important tools!
  • It's a Real Job: While it may be unpaid or for credit, the responsibilities are real. Show up prepared, professional, and ready to engage.
